The Problem

Every aerial vehicle shares
the same fundamental flaw.

Helicopters, drones, eVTOLs, and jet engines all generate thrust by displacing air downward. This creates rotor wash, turbulence, noise, and dust that limit where these vehicles can operate.

Rotor wash exclusion zones

eVTOL companies spend $10M to $50M per vertiport to manage wind. Delivery drones cannot land on porches. Film drones blow actors' hair.

Brownout kills operators

In Iraq and Afghanistan, dust clouds from helicopter rotor wash caused more aircraft losses than enemy fire. DOD spends over $1B annually on mitigation.

Firefighting drones fan flames

No existing drone can approach an active wildfire. Rotor wash scatters burning embers, the primary mechanism of wildfire spread.

Rotors fail in vacuum

Propellers require atmosphere. Mars helicopter Ingenuity barely flew in 1% air density. In space, only rockets work, and they require fuel.

The Solution

Centrifugal force. Directed.

Two pneumatic tires rotate in opposite directions, each driven by a roller of equal diameter. A transmission belt under tension connects them. The counter-rotation generates directional centrifugal force, producing thrust without displacing a single molecule of air.

Zero downdraft

No air displacement means no rotor wash, no dust, no turbulence. Hover silently one meter from a person.

Medium agnostic

Centrifugal force is mechanical, not aerodynamic. Works identically in air, water, and vacuum.

Scales to any size

From 6 inch inspection drones to passenger aircraft. Same physics at every scale.

Electric native

Designed for electric motor drive. Solar powered in space, battery powered on Earth. No combustion required.

How It Works

Physics, not aerodynamics.

The mechanism is elegant. Counter-rotating tires generate directional centrifugal force through synchronized mechanical interaction.

Counter-rotating tires cancel lateral forces. Net centrifugal force is directed upward through the swivel axis.

STEP 01

Counter rotation

Two pneumatic tires of equal diameter rotate in opposite directions. Rollers pressed against each tire provide drive force.

STEP 02

Belt tension

A transmission belt connects the two discs under tension, synchronizing speed and ensuring equal and opposite lateral forces cancel out.

STEP 03

Centrifugal force generation

Each rotating tire generates centrifugal force outward. The counter-rotation geometry directs the net force vector in a single chosen direction.

STEP 04

Directional thrust

A swivel at the center of gravity rotates the entire ensemble, steering the predominant force in any direction. Zero air displacement.

Key Principle
F = m · v² / r

Centrifugal force depends only on mass, velocity, and radius. No medium required. Works in air, water, or vacuum.
